# Missed-Call Buyer Pain Research

Question:
Is missed-call recovery a real buyer pain for home service businesses?

Short answer:
Yes. The pain is simple: calls become jobs, and missed calls become lost jobs.

Best buyer:
Home service contractors with existing inbound demand.

Best niches:

- HVAC
- plumbing
- roofing
- electrical
- remodelers
- cleaners
- landscapers

Why this is painful:

- Owners are often working in the field.
- Customers expect fast answers.
- If nobody answers, customers call the next company.
- Businesses may already be paying for ads, so missed calls waste ad spend.
- The value of one booked job can cover the tool.

Useful buyer language:

- "You already paid to make the phone ring."
- "We make sure someone answers."
- "Stop losing jobs to voicemail."
- "Every missed call is a job you may never know about."

Competitor signals:

- Rivety sells AI agents that answer, qualify, book, and confirm contractor leads.
- Rivr positions around "Never miss a call" and "book more jobs automatically."
- ServiceTitan has an AI Voice Agent for trades/service businesses.
- Ruby sells call answering/reception for small businesses, showing the old human-service version of the same pain.

Risks:

- Some published missed-call numbers are vendor marketing claims.
- We should not overstate exact stats in sales copy unless we can verify the client's own call logs.
- Best sales move is a free missed-call audit using the buyer's own numbers.
